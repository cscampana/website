Latinx Leadership Program

I was part of a highly selective leadership development program at the University of California, San Diego. It selects twelve students from Latinx backgrounds to participate in a year-long program.
There were many opportunities to develop new skills and improve communication abilities. One of the key highlights of this program was the one-to-one mentorship with a highly sought-after venture capitalist during its duration. Another highlight was the practicum developed with Startup San Diego, a 501c3 organization. Alongside two other students, Caike developed a guide to streamline the production of events. In sum, this experience was fundamental for improving communication and leadership abilities.

About Me

In an era of constant technological flux, many innovative revolutions are happening all around us; I was born. Still, that was over 25 years ago, in SÃ£o Paulo, Brazil. The internet had just arrived in my country. Amid all that revolution, I knew it would be the future since my early childhood.

Childhood 

I would spend countless hours in front of the computer during my upbringing. One day, playing games, I became more curious regarding how it works behind the scenes. I researched and begged my father to enroll me in a course; I was 9 years old. It was a self-paced in-person course, and this was in 2007. The course was a general introduction to computing and its technologies. I finished and "graduated" from it. This period was fundamental for me. I somewhat comprehended how computers worked and learned to code for web 1.0.

Work, Work, Reward

Fast-forwarding to my mid-teens, I was about to graduate from high school, but I had no prospects. I was just lost in the world. I decided to pursue my passion and took a yearlong Oracle certified Java course at Global code. At the end of this course, I graduated from high school. Shortly after, I got hired by International Business Machines as a Helpdesk analyst. After a year, I got hired by Hewlett Packard Enterprise due to an account change.
But I felt that I worked for the benefit of a corporation to aid its profit goal and not for the greater marginalized society. My goal was always to promote the inclusion of my community and destroy barriers towards the access of technologies in ways to create a bridge between the gas. Thus, I decided to quit and pursue a higher educational degree. 

Unique opportunities, new challenges

I had an opportunity to come to the United States to study, and I knew I had to grab it. Leaving all my roots, friends, and family behind, I embarked on this journey. At first, I enrolled at the San Diego Mesa community college. During that time, I became even more confident that my path was the right one; After graduating with an Associate of Arts in Applied Math with high honors. I transferred to the University of California, San Diego, and am currently pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Mathematics-Computer Science.
